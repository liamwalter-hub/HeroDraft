How to Play

The Five Modes

Open Draft — you and the CPU take turns picking freely from the full roster in a snake draft.
Fate Draft — each pick you’re shown one random hero per role. Take it or pass for a new offer. You work with what fate deals.
Gauntlet Mode — build your team freely, then fight five fixed legendary teams in sequence. HP resets between stages.
Fate Gauntlet — draft once in Fate Draft, then fight an endless series of CPU-drafted teams until your fixed roster falls. How long can you last?
Daily Challenge — one Fate or Open Draft per day with a rotating constraint. One official attempt. Win streaks tracked across days.

Roles

Leader Buffs all allies each round — scales with Leadership and tier. A legendary leader buffs significantly harder than a human one.
Hero Your all-rounder — damage from Strength, Intelligence, and Agility. Cosmic heroes like Superman operate at a completely different level.
Defender Shields your most wounded ally every round and intercepts incoming hits. Damage uses Strength, Durability, and Intelligence.
Warrior Your main damage dealer — Strength and Agility only. Bonus damage vs heroes. Cosmic warriors like Thanos can end battles alone.
Support Heals your most wounded hero each round and debuffs the biggest enemy threat. Healing scales on Intelligence — Yoda heals far more than Tyrion.

Power Tiers

Every hero has a power tier that multiplies their damage, healing, buffs, and HP.

■ Human ×1.00 — peak human
◈ Enhanced ×1.15 — superhuman, magical
★ Legendary ×1.30 — gods, dark lords
◆ Cosmic ×1.50 — planetary threats

Each team is limited to 1 cosmic and a combined total of 3 legendary or cosmic heroes. Both limits apply equally to both sides.

Fury & Rivals

When a hero faces their rival in battle they enter a fury state — a damage bonus scaling with that rival’s tier. Human +10%, enhanced +15%, legendary +22%, cosmic +30%, stacking up to +60%. Thanos facing Thor alone hits +30%. With T’Challa and Spider-Man also present, +55%.

Rivals on your own side carry an 18% betrayal risk each round — no fury bonus, only danger. The alignment panel before battle shows all active bonuses and warnings.

Synergies & Alignment

Heroes from the same universe fight better together (+10% per same-universe ally, uncapped). Named ally bonds add a further +15% — Yoda with Luke heals far more than Yoda alone.

Evil heroes inspire each other (+10% per evil ally, capped at +20%). Good heroes bond similarly. Mixing alignments hurts both sides.

Armies

Every team fields an army unit dealing splash damage to the enemy each round. Field a leader with their own army for a +20% damage bonus to that leader while the army lives. The affinity is worth chasing — but if you can’t match it, take the strongest available army instead.

Draft Grade

After completing your draft, your team receives a grade from D to S based on tier power, universe synergies, named ally bonds, alignment cohesion, army affinity, and rival risk. In Fate Draft, it also shows the best grade achievable from the offers you were shown — so you can tell if fate dealt you a weak hand or if you left value on the table.

Gauntlet & Clones

In Gauntlet Mode, if any of your heroes appear on an enemy stage roster, they fight as clones at −25% damage that stage. Finding a five-hero team that clears all five stages without cloning is the real challenge.

In Fate Gauntlet, the CPU drafts a fresh legal team each stage. Difficulty escalates — easy in the early stages, hard by stage five. Your personal best is tracked on the homepage.

Daily Challenge

Each day has two constraints — a draft mode (Open or Fate) and a special rule such as No Gods (no cosmic heroes), Enhanced Only (every hero must be enhanced tier), Sworn Enemies (your team must contain a rivalry pair), or Iron Draft (human and enhanced only). Your first attempt counts toward your win streak. Retries are unranked.
