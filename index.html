How to Play

HeroDraft has two modes. In Draft Mode you and the CPU take turns picking heroes in a snake draft — one pick, then the other side gets two, and so on. In Gauntlet Mode you build your team freely and fight five preset opponents back to back.

Roles

Leader Buffs the whole team at the start of each round. A strong leader makes everyone hit harder.
Hero Your all-rounder — solid damage, balanced across strength, intelligence, and agility.
Defender Shields your most wounded ally every round and intercepts hits aimed at teammates.
Warrior Hits hardest. Bonus damage vs heroes, harder to intercept. Your main damage dealer.
Support Heals your most wounded hero each round and debuffs the biggest threat on the enemy side.

Pay attention to universe synergies — heroes from the same world fight better together. Ally bonds between named pairs give further bonuses. Be wary of rivals: a hero who hates a teammate has an 18% chance to betray them mid-battle instead of attacking the enemy.

Each team also fields an army unit — soldiers who deal splash damage to the whole enemy team every round. Pick one with leader affinity for a bonus.

Gauntlet Mode

Pick your roster once and fight five legendary teams in sequence. HP resets fully between stages — but your team is locked for the entire run. Stages escalate in difficulty, with the most dominant team in the multiverse waiting at Stage 5.

There's a catch: if any of your heroes appear on that stage's enemy roster, they're treated as clones and deal 25% less damage that fight. Copying the final boss team gets you to Stage 5 — it won't get you through it. Finding the team that can clear all five is the real challenge.
